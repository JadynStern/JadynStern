About Radiohead

Radiohead are an English rock band formed in Abingdon, Oxfordshire, in 1985. The band consists of Thom Yorke, brothers Jonny and Colin Greenwood, Ed O'Brien, and Philip Selway. They have worked with producer Nigel Godrich and cover artist Stanley Donwood since 1994.

Known for their experimental approach to rock music, Radiohead have released critically acclaimed albums including "OK Computer," "Kid A," and "In Rainbows." Their music has evolved from alternative rock to incorporate electronic music, classical music, and jazz influences.

Band Members

Thom Yorke

Lead Vocals, Guitar, Piano

The primary lyricist and lead vocalist of Radiohead, Thom Yorke's distinctive voice and introspective lyrics have become the band's signature. He also pursues solo projects and collaborations.

Jonny Greenwood

Lead Guitar, Keyboards

Known for his innovative guitar work and electronic experimentation, Jonny is also an accomplished film composer, having scored multiple films for director Paul Thomas Anderson.

Colin Greenwood

Bass Guitar

Jonny's older brother, Colin provides the rhythmic foundation with his bass playing. His melodic bass lines are integral to Radiohead's sound.

Ed O'Brien

Guitar, Backing Vocals

Ed's guitar work and backing vocals add texture and depth to Radiohead's music. He also released his debut solo album "Earth" in 2020.

Philip Selway

Drums, Percussion

The band's drummer since its formation, Philip's precise and creative drumming style has been crucial to Radiohead's evolution. He has also released solo albums.
